Frequently Asked Questions

What is a polygraph test, and how does it work?

A polygraph test, commonly known as a lie detector test, measures physiological responses such as heart rate, blood pressure, respiration, and skin conductivity while a person answers questions. By analyzing these responses, polygraph examiners assess truthfulness.

Are polygraph tests accurate?

Polygraph tests are highly accurate when conducted by trained professionals. However, no test can be 100% accurate. Polygraph results are typically used as supportive evidence rather than definitive proof of truthfulness.

How long does a polygraph test take?

The entire process generally takes between 1 to 2 hours. This includes a pre-test interview, the polygraph test itself, and a post-test discussion of the results.

Are the results of a polygraph test admissible in court?

In many jurisdictions, polygraph test results may not be admissible in court. It is advised to consult with legal counsel to understand how polygraph evidence may apply to your specific case.

Can nervousness affect the test results?

While everyone may experience some level of nervousness, a trained polygraph examiner is skilled at distinguishing normal anxiety from responses that indicate deception.

Who will see my polygraph results?

Your results are kept confidential and shared only with the individuals you authorize. Verity Polygraph Services LLC takes privacy and confidentiality very seriously.

What should I do to prepare for a polygraph test?

Get a good night’s sleep, avoid excessive caffeine, and try to stay calm. The examiner will provide additional instructions prior to the test.
